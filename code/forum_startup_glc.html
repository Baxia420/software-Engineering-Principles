Need advice: Choosing between Startup vs GLC for internship

#career-choice #internship-hunting
forum 42 Replies
KW
Khalid Waleed Issa
Posted 2 days ago • Computer Science, Year 3

Hi everyone, I'm currently in my 3rd year of Computer Science and I've been fortunate enough to receive two internship offers for the upcoming semester. However, I'm really torn between the two and could use some perspective from alumni or seniors who have been in similar situations.

Offer 1: Fast-growing Tech Startup in KL
They are building an AI-driven logistics platform. The team is small (around 20 people) and I would be working directly with the CTO. The pros are obvious: steep learning curve, real ownership of features, and a modern tech stack (React, Node, AWS). Cons: slightly lower allowance, potentially chaotic unstructured environment.

Offer 2: Major Government Linked Company (GLC)
This is for their internal IT department. Pros: Strong brand name on the resume, highly structured 12-week mentorship program, better allowance, and potential for a return offer post-graduation. Cons: Legacy tech stack (mostly Java/Spring), slower pace, and I might just be doing minor bug fixes rather than building new things.

My long-term goal is to eventually become a software architect or technical lead. Does the "prestige" of a GLC outweigh the hands-on experience of a startup early in one's career? Any advice is appreciated!

I was in this exact situation 3 years ago! I chose the startup route and didn't regret it. The learning curve was brutal, but when I graduated, I had actual shipped products to talk about in interviews. The GLC name is nice, but in pure software engineering, technical skills and actual project impact usually win out in the private sector.

Counter-point to Sarah: While startups give you breadth, GLCs give you depth in process. You learn how enterprise software is actually maintained, deployed, and scaled securely. Plus, the network you build in a GLC internship can be invaluable if you want to stay in the local corporate ecosystem.
