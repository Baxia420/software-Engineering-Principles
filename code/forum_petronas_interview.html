Petronas Software Engineering Interview Experience - 2026

ZS
Zay Yar Shin
Posted 2 days ago • 8 min read
#interview-prep #petronas #software-engineering

Hi everyone, I recently completed the interview process for the Software Engineering Internship position at Petronas (KLCC HQ) for the 2026 intake. I wanted to share my experience here since past posts really helped me prepare.

Stage 1: Initial Screening (Video Recording)

This was an asynchronous video interview via HireVue. You get 3 questions, with 30 seconds to prepare and 2 minutes to record your answer for each.

  • Introduce yourself and why you chose Software Engineering.
  • Describe a time you had to work with a difficult team member.
  • Why Petronas and how does our core values align with yours?

Tip: Keep it structured using the STAR method. They really value candidates who understand their work culture.

Stage 2: Technical Assessment (HackerRank)

About a week after the screening, I received an invitation for a 90-minute HackerRank test. It consisted of:

  • 10 Multiple Choice Questions (Data Structures, OOP concepts, basic Networking).
  • 2 Coding Challenges (Medium difficulty). One was array manipulation, the other involved writing a simple SQL query using JOINs.

I managed to pass all test cases for the first coding question but only partial for the second. It seems they look at your logic and code structure rather than just a perfect score.

Stage 3: Final Interview with Lead Engineer

This was a 45-minute Microsoft Teams interview with a Senior Engineering Manager and an HR representative. It was surprisingly conversational.

  • Technical: We went over my resume. They asked deep dive questions into a React project I built, specifically how I managed state and handled API errors.
  • System Design (Lite): I was asked how I would approach building a dashboard to display real-time sensor data from oil rigs. Didn't need to write code, just draw a high-level architecture diagram using excalidraw.
  • Behavioral: Questions about handling tight deadlines and learning new technologies quickly.

I got the offer email 2 weeks after the final round! Feel free to ask any questions below. Good luck to everyone applying!
